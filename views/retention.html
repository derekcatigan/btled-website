Retention Policy

Retention guidelines and standards for BTLEd & BEED programs at SLSU-FETI.

Overview of Retention Policy (BTLEd & BEED)

The retention policy of the Teacher Education Programs (BTLEd and BEED) at Southern Leyte State University ensures that students are qualified to continue in the program through a series of evaluations. First-year students are assessed based on three components: qualifying examination (30%), panel interview (30%), and academic performance or GPA (40%). The qualifying exam is standardized and conducted face-to-face, while the panel interview uses rubrics to evaluate students' communication skills and readiness to become teachers.

For higher year levels, second-year students must take another qualifying exam and interview, while third-year students undergo a LET-like examination covering general education, professional education, and major subjects. These assessments aim to ensure that students are academically prepared and competent for the teaching profession.

Guide Questions

  1. What are the components used to evaluate first-year students? Qualifying exam, panel interview, and GPA.
  2. How much percentage is assigned to each component? Qualifying exam – 30%, Panel interview – 30%, GPA – 40%.
  3. When is the qualifying examination conducted? It is conducted before the final examination in the second semester.
  4. What is the purpose of the panel interview? To assess the student's communication skills, personality, and readiness to become a teacher.
  5. What type of exam is given to second-year students? A qualifying exam with multiple-choice questions and a panel interview.
  6. What is the LET-like examination for third-year students? An exam similar to the Licensure Examination for Teachers to assess readiness.
  7. What subjects are included in the LET-like exam? General Education (20%), Professional Education (40%), and Major Subjects (40%).
  8. Why is the retention policy important? It ensures that only qualified, competent, and prepared students continue in the teacher education program.
