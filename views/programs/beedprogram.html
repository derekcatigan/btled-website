Education Program

Bachelor of Elementary Education (BEED)

Prepares students to become skilled, compassionate, and innovative elementary educators through hands-on teaching and educational theory.

Program Overview

Back

Bachelor of Elementary Education (BEED) is a professional degree program designed to develop competent and effective teachers for the elementary level. The program emphasizes instructional strategies, curriculum design, classroom management, and field-based teaching experiences. Students gain both theoretical knowledge and practical skills to foster learning, critical thinking, and holistic development in young learners.

Course Curriculum

Year Core Focus Key Subjects
1st Year Foundations of Education Introduction to Education, Child and Adolescent Development, General Psychology, Communication Skills, Foundations of Curriculum and Instruction.
2nd Year Pedagogical Skills Educational Technology, Principles of Teaching, Assessment and Evaluation, Language Arts and Literacy Development, Mathematics and Science for Elementary.
3rd Year Classroom Practice & Integration Curriculum Design, Classroom Management, Educational Research Methods, Teaching Internship I, Arts and Music in Education.
4th Year Professional Immersion Teaching Internship II, Advanced Classroom Strategies, Inclusive Education, Action Research / Capstone Project, Leadership in Education.
