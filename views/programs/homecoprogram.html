Education Program

Home Economics

Develops hands-on competencies in culinary arts, hospitality services, and family life skills.

Program Overview

Back

Home Economics is a technical–vocational program that focuses on developing practical life and employable skills in food preparation, hospitality, clothing and textile care, caregiving, and household management. The program emphasizes hands-on training, entrepreneurship, and service-oriented work, preparing students for employment, self-employment, or further studies in home economics–related fields.

Hands-on Food and Household Skills Training

Guided Practical Workshops and Demonstrations

Course Curriculum

Year Core Focus Key Subjects
1st Year Foundations of Home Economics Introduction to Home Economics, Food and Nutrition Basics, Clothing and Textile Fundamentals, Household Management, Safety and Sanitation.
2nd Year Technical Core Skills Culinary Arts and Baking, Basic Sewing and Textile Craft, Child and Elderly Care, Home-based Entrepreneurship, Food Service Operations.
3rd Year Applied & Specialized Practices Advanced Cooking and Catering, Hospitality and Tourism Fundamentals, Consumer Education, Event Planning and Management, Internship / Practicum I.
4th Year Professional Immersion Capstone Project (Catering, Hospitality, or Care Services), Internship / Practicum II, Family Resource Management, Community Extension Work, Emerging Trends in Home Economics.
