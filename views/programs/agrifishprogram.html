Agriculture & Fishery Arts

Prepares students with hands-on knowledge in farming, aquaculture, and modern agricultural practices.

Program Overview

Back

Agriculture & Fishery Arts is a technical–vocational track that equips students with practical knowledge and hands-on skills in farming, animal production, and fisheries. The program focuses on sustainable agricultural practices, food production, and the use of modern tools and techniques to support local and national food systems. It prepares learners for entrepreneurship, employment in agri-based industries, or further studies in agriculture and fisheries.

Course Curriculum

Year Core Focus Key Subjects
1st Year Agricultural Foundations Introduction to Agriculture & Fisheries, basic crop production, soil science, farm tools and safety, and environmental science.
2nd Year Technical Core Skills Livestock and poultry production, aquaculture techniques, pest and disease management, agricultural mechanics, and farm management basics.
3rd Year Applied & Sustainable Practices Sustainable and organic farming, advanced aquaculture, post-harvest technology, agribusiness and entrepreneurship, and research methods.
4th Year Professional Immersion Internship / on-the-job training, community agricultural extension, capstone project (farm or fishery system), and industry practice.
