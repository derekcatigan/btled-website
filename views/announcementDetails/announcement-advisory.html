Suspension of Classes Due to Weather Conditions

January 5, 2026 FTVE Office

The Faculty of Technical and Vocational Education (FTVE) announces the suspension of all face-to-face classes on January 5, 2026, due to the continuous heavy rainfall and unfavorable weather conditions affecting Southern Leyte.

This measure is taken to prioritize the safety of students, faculty, and staff, especially those traveling from nearby municipalities. The university continues to closely monitor official weather advisories and local government announcements.

Class and Campus Operations

  • Face-to-face classes: Suspended for the day
  • Online classes: May proceed at the discretion of subject instructors
  • Administrative offices: Operating with limited staff or subject to further notice

Student Guidelines

Students are advised to stay at home, avoid unnecessary travel, and follow safety instructions issued by local authorities. Those residing in high-risk areas are encouraged to take extra precautions.

Academic concerns such as missed lessons, assessments, and submissions will be addressed by instructors once regular classes resume. Students are advised to communicate directly with their respective teachers for further instructions.

Updates regarding the resumption of classes will be announced through official FTVE communication channels. Please remain informed by regularly checking the university website and official social media pages.
